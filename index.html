Problems With Ipe

Ipe is a dense hardwood that will usually require annual maintenance to keep it looking in top shape. Companies have developed stains/oils that have the ability to penetrate or adhere to Ipe. When using these products on new or properly cleaned Ipe, they will look great and usually give an amber/natural appearance, such as when the Ipe has water sprayed on it.

However, over time, these products will cause problems. After a year, or at most two, an Ipe deck in the elements will have to be retreated. The previous coating will have to be removed, or in most cases, the deck is power washed, and usually, a good amount of the previous product still remains. Decks are then usually recoated with the same or similar product.

When this happens, you will usually have an uneven appearance because of areas of bare wood and areas that still had the previous coating. You will also have issues with the new coating drying as it sits on top of the previous coating. The previous coating will try to repel the new coating as it would rain or any type of moisture sitting on your deck. Eventually, this will cause heavy mildew growth, almost turning the deck black.

Many Ipe decks have railings, fascia, and stair risers made of other woods. Whether it's Redwood or Cedar, they are softer woods. The stains/oils developed for Ipe are usually for exotic hardwoods, and when used on softer woods, will turn them black with heavy mildew growth, even though they will look nice when the wood is new or freshly cleaned.

Full Deck Restoration

When an Ipe deck has been coated multiple times with hardwood stains or oils, restoring it to its natural beauty requires a complete stripping down to the bare wood. Hardwood stains formulated for Ipe are notoriously difficult to remove, and simple power washing often proves insufficient. Excessive force from power washing risks damaging the deck, making a chemical stripper the best option. This method softens the old coatings, allowing effective removal through power washing.

After the old coatings are stripped, a neutralizing agent is applied and scrubbed into the wood to counteract the stripper and brighten the surface. Once the deck is thoroughly dry, all areas are sanded to eliminate any residual stains and to smooth raised wood fibers caused by stripping and power washing.

Finally, we apply two coats of our eco-friendly, water-based hybrid oil, imported from Germany and Denmark, to all hardwood areas. For sections of the deck constructed with softer woods, a specially formulated hybrid oil is used to ensure optimal results and lasting protection.

Yearly Maintenance Plans

Due to the dense nature of Ipe and other hardwoods, an Ipe deck, especially one exposed to full sunlight, typically does not retain its stained appearance for more than a single summer season. As a hardwood deck owner, it’s essential to have a maintenance plan that avoids the need for frequent stripping and sanding whenever restaining is required.

The oils we use provide a full season of enjoyment, even in direct sunlight. After a year, your deck will still maintain a good appearance, though we recommend recoating before too much of the oil wears away. One of the key benefits of our products is that your deck can be cleaned using scrub brushes and recoated over the existing stain. With regular maintenance, your Ipe deck can retain its beauty for many years to come.
